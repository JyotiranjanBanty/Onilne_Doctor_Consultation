Flu (Influenza): A contagious respiratory illness caused by viruses. Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, and fatigue.

Diabetes: A chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. Symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, and fatigue.

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure): A condition where the force of blood against the artery walls is too high. Symptoms may include headaches, dizziness, and nosebleeds.

Asthma: A chronic condition affecting the airways in the lungs, causing breathing difficulties, coughing, and wheezing.

Arthritis: A joint disorder causing pain, stiffness, and swelling. Common in older adults.
